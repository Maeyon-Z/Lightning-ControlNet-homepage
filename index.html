Lightning-ControlNet :Integrating Lightning Control into Text-to-Image Diffusion Models

Mengyuan Zhang, Gang Li, Tingting Chai, Yuanrong Xu, Xin Sun, Zhengqian Zhang, Guanglu Zhou,
The Faculty of Computing, Harbin Institute of Technology
Institute of Software, Chinese Academy of Sciences

Abstract

Text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models have attracted considerable attention due to their ability to generate diverse and highly realistic images. However, achieving finer-grained control, such as depth-to-image and semantic-to-image generation, remains a significant challenge. While advanced methods like ControlNet and Uni-ControlNet have shown promise in addressing this challenge, we argue that, since the pre-trained model already integrates text prompts, it is unnecessary for the control branch to further incorporate text prompts through cross-attention layers. In this paper, we propose a more lightweight yet effective control branch named Lightning-ControlNet, which eliminates the cross-attention layers in ControlNet and efficiently incorporates spatial features from the condition images through Spatial Normalization. Lightning-ControlNet requires only one-time training by adopting a unified process to handle multimodal condition images, differing from the majority of earlier approaches that necessitate distinct control branch training for each type of conditioning image. Moreover, we propose Time-Division-Control (TDC) to balance controllability and generation quality. TDC relies on the control branch for fine-grained spatial control in the initial steps of the entire multi-step sampling process, while in the later steps, it solely depends on the pre-trained model to optimize the details of generated images. Both TDC and pre-trained Lightning-ControlNet can serve as plug-and-play components to act on other related methods. Extensive experiments demonstrate our method is either superior to or on par with state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods, despite a significant reduction in parameter count.

Lightning-ControlNet Framework

We first conduct ablation experiments to verify approaches like ControlNet, where the control branch still receives text prompts despite the pre-trained model already receiving them, are unnecessary. Upon this discovery, we eliminates the cross-attention layers in ControlNet and devised a SpatialResBlock to construct Lightning-ControlNet. It can be visually observed that Lightning-ControlNet has a more streamlined and lightweight structure.

Due to our meticulous structural design and uniformization of different type of conditioning images, Lightning-ControlNet has significant advantages over previous methods in terms of parameters and fine-tuning costs.

Structure Details

The Feature Extractor captures features at vari- ous resolutions from conditioning images and channels them into SpatialResBlocks A to E correspondingly. SpatialResBlock E aligns with the Middle Block of the frozen U-Net, where its output features are directly integrated with the frozen Encoder’s output and passed as input to the Middle Block. On the other hand, SpatialResBlocks A through D correspond to the first block of each stage of the frozen Decoder. Their output features are concatenated with skip connections to be utilized in the denoising process.
